Outtakes of my creative writing portfolio from 2015 - 2017.

Name

I think it is red, rich and bold. It’s sweet to hear people saying my name with their accents, hear their voices stretch out its syllables. I love the way my grandmother says ‘Katharine’. My name does not mean hope or happiness, but I have filled it with good traits that belong to me. I think ‘Katharines’ have been given their name by pure luck. A mere trawling through the abyss of the English language and finding something which seems familiar. Sundays are the sweetest day of the week. There is always soft background music, the smell of fresh laundry. I always think of my name with the fondness I think of the weekend, of Sundays. 

Mia + Boys

The boys you fell in love with were like actors. They were never as exciting as the roles they played. Mia knew this, but she went for them anyway. Because in some twisted way, to hurt created excellence in her body and mind. Her words became polished and beautiful. Her skin smoother, plumper. She became youthful, her eyes wider, lashes slicker, hair healthier, nails stronger. The thought itself carried a weight over her.

Suburbia

I
Dark suburbia is scary. It's different from Hollywood films and pastel skies - more dangerous too. The glamorous suburbs filled with stylish women who cry themselves to sleep at night - that is what we want. We want Xanax and coke to ease the pain - it's scary to think that the woman working three jobs can just end herself. The drugs and the lights dull almost compared to red lipstick of the new wife. Oh, how should she pay for such a divorce, what glamour! What beauty - a tarnished nickel in the water. A failed daughter’s cry-baby drug boy sicko killing his baby girl.

II
An eye for an eye, I’m scared of consequences. So, I watch my shadow and step, avoid the pavement and keep an eye down, my iris circling out. Crying through the veil of tears and watching for a bullet to come through my head. I hear noises at night - a motorbike, howl hoot and bark from the animals - why should they yell at night? What is in our suburbs that make us cry? Pageant queen smiles at me - why the placid look? I don't like her, she doesn’t like me, what else is there to say with that look?

Womanhood

My life is a rotation of blood, bleeding and revitalisation. When I feel dirty, I have the urge to purify myself. Aquify myself in a ritual. Sometimes I break, upon and upon myself. But I’m not sad, I’m not sad, and I convince myself that that is normal. Bloated, fat and then emaciated. Cream coloured paper filled with these thoughts.

Beauty Regime II

I’ve recently become obsessed with drinking water. I drink close to 6-7 large cups of water a day because it will make my skin perfect. This idea now defines my idea of beauty – natural. Natural beauty is what will separate me from other girls. I use a thin layer of cream on my face every morning. Recently, I have started getting larger bumps on my skin, my nose is dryer and slightly peeling and I’m not sure how to stop this. I read an article that suggested that oils might moisturise as supposed to saturate your natural skin, but oils feel greasy and summer is approaching. I firmly believe heels are essential in the beauty mantra. Seven-inch, six-inch are all fine - they elongate the leg, straighten the back and make the chin better swooped.

American
Dark room. Two rows of “U” shaped tables. Our heroine cries silently, melodramatically. She is an actor. The heroine bats her lashes in the dark cinema.  A jour de jeune, Matthew smokes into the dark. It is an almost overwhelming beauty, the ugliest can transform into the most extraordinary. There is so much sensation which has been blessed upon the wayward earth. Rose, with Rose. After an afternoon of debauchery and sin, cigar, black jack playing, our heart throb graces the room with supernatural awe and grotesque splendour. Matthew leans over and he stamps out his cigar into the ashtray of porcelain, it is shaped like a rude ballet dancer fanning out her skirt.

Rio

There is an aesthetic to poverty – our ‘thin’ a trait that is inspired by the swaths of thin limbs and the pile of bones that stretch the coloured skin of peoples in the world. There is an aesthetic to wealth - a mixture of luxury, gold, Cartier status on one's’ arm and a sway to one's leather coat, boots, hair, skin - tantalising. From the cartels in Mexico I find my way, my swish and rumba to the lights, I sway and wonder whether there are good men in Rio – Rio the party the light the fire in my glass, rumbling down my throat a tidal breeze. A man can pursue the arts in the city of god, a man can be who he wants to be and he can cry out to those who will listen.

Jazz

My eyelids are so fat and heavy that my pupil is a slit but I see her – all golden and taut and tan. Holier than thou sort of girl. She isn’t hardened like the women I know – the Mishkas’ cold and silent in stony realisation, the women who endure hardship, labour and brutality. She was a little girl, soft, buttery tissues tearing. Her laugh is wispy as I tell her that she is an angel. So light it skims the fibers of my body, anchoring on heavy. She dances, disappears. Mishka and Getty sway me away asking about the music, the drugs, the poetry but I can only answer with a “darn” or “yeah”, I run back. I am absolutely besotted, flushed and I am in love again with an Angel with rosy lips and oily skin. This entire night has been a blur, I am drunk on four-dollar scotches, crying in Mishka’s lap and I can only think of her.
